01
Every cannabis brand was borrowing from the pharmacy or the street corner. Neither talked to the actual experience.

The category felt over-coded in two directions: clinical utility or underground rebellion. What was missing was guidance for people who were actually trying to feel something specific.

Research
02
The brief was not to make cannabis feel safer, trendier, or louder. It was to make it legible. People needed a way to understand what a strain might feel like before decoding names, formats, and THC percentages.
"The problem wasn't awareness.
The problem was navigation."

04 - Vision
Choose by how you want to feel. Not by what it's called.

The interface reorganized discovery around mood states — letting people enter through experience rather than product jargon.

Hero screen · Kindling web
The vision was to build an interface that felt more like a guide than a catalogue. Each mood became its own small promise — a wayfinding tool, a tonal cue, and a visual state all at once.
"How do you want to feel?"
